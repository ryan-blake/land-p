    • Shop Shotguns:

    Early Histroy:

    A smoothbore shoulder weapon for firing shots at short ranges. The modern market for shotguns features varieties of actions, calibers, chambering and barrel lengths. Used in hunting to sporting, a timeless firearm that secures itself in a specific niche, the shotgun is primarily used for hitting moving targets. The loads in the shells are yet another field of versatility of its own, packed light ample for small birds, heavy enough to take a deer, to one solid shot to hunt big game.

    Shop Pump-Action

    Pump-action: One in which the handgrip can be pumped back and forth in order to eject a spent round of ammunition and to chamber a fresh shell. John Browning is credited for making on of the first pump shotguns in 1897, originally intended for military use in trench warfare, earning the name “trench gun”. Today, the pump action is known to be simple, and reliable, suitable for sporting, hunting, and home defense. The Mossberg M-590 currently is issued to the military, the Remington 870 for the police. Pump shotguns are still a viable choice of weapon for the military, police, sportsman, and hunter alike. Browning makes the BPS, of which is built more for the hunter, but is yet another good choice in home defense.

    Shop Semi-automatic

    Semi-automatic A semi-automatic is able to fire repeatedly but requiring release and another pressure of the trigger for each successive shot Extremely popular among every field of use, its presence ranges from target shooting, to military breeching scenarios. Currently the military uses the Benelli M4 as the standard semi-auto shotgun. Clay shooters will argue which shotgun is the best pick in the semi-automatic category, but the best sellers of 2014 were the Mossberg 930, Remington 11-87, Bora BR 99, Browning auto-5, and Remington 1100. Other popular companies are, Berretta, Weatherby, FN, Winchester, Tri Star, Win Gun, and Century Arms. Each of them come in variants suited for specific tasks.

    Shop Double Barrel / Over Under

    Double Barrel / Over Under: universally break open actions, with the barrels tilting up at the rear to expose the breech ends of the barrels for unloading and reloading The first Breech loading shotguns were side by side shotguns that were in black powder. Later on the over under system became a competitor to the side by side. Typically, not chambered in lengths above 3 inch chambers, is still a good shotgun, considering that the 2 ¾ inch shell is known to ample to do the job. Companies like Century arms, and Mossberg still make the side by side shotgun. In over under markets, the companies American Tactical Imports, Beretta, Browning, CZ, Howa, Maverick arms, Mossberg, Tristar, Weatherby, and Winchester. Popular in clay shooting, and the Olympics, the over-under has become the go to for competition shooting, and hunting alike.
