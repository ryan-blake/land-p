    Pistols

    A small firearm designed to be held in one hand. Though there are many types of actions for the pistol, the two that dominate in popularity, are the revolver, and semi auto systems. The revolver was the first practical repeating pistol, it was not out fazed in the military until 1917. Soon after the 1911 became standard for the military, being the countries first standard issue semi-auto pistol for the military. Despite this, it wasn’t until the advent of the wonder 9’s, was the revolver out fazed by the police as standard issue. Semi-auto pistols have taken the reigns for military and police since then.

    Shop Revolver

    Revolver a repeating handgun that has a revolving cylinder containing multiple chambers and at least one barrel for firing. The revolver still remains a viable option as a Concealed Carry, one major reason being the weapon’s reliability. The revolver has two types of actions, single and double action. The single action requires pulling the hammer back to rotate the cylinder to cock the gun. Double action is when the action of pulling the trigger rotates the cylinder and fires the revolver. The snub nose 38 Special has been choice of concealed carry for a long time, and still is one of the most purchased revolvers. Revolvers come in various calibers ranging from 22 short, to 44magnum, and larger. Companies like, Taylors & Co., Heritage, Ruger , Traditions, and European armory continue to make single action revolvers. For double action, some good companies include, Ruger, Smith & Wesson , and Taurus.

    Shop Semi-auto

    Semi auto:Semi-auto weapons/pistols are able to fire repeatedly but require release and another pressure of the trigger for each successive shot. The semi-auto pistol has been in use by the Military since 1911, when colt released the Colt 1911, in 45acp. Today there are countless calibers, sizes, and materials that make these guns. Among the most popular companies producing semi automatic handguns are, Ruger, Smith & Wesson, Sig Sauer, Heckler & Koch, Glock, Springfield, and Beretta.
